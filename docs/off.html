The Carwash Caper

I decided to turn the car off.

The ventilation down in the underground is surprisingly good, so I never worry about exhaust fumes, but I also had no idea when that lady would actually be back. I walked over to the driver's side, yanking the hose around behind me so it wouldn't get stuck on anything, and I grabbed the door handle to pull it open. As the door slid toward me, I crouched inside and turned the key off.

For just a moment, I took a peek around the interior, taking note of the sleek leather seats and fancy doohickeys all over the dashboard. Of even greater interest were the stacks of suitcases laid on the back seats. Compelled by my own curiousity, I reached over and unlatched one of them, and it was filled with $100 bills. I could feel my eyes grow wide in surprise—perhaps mixed with a little bit of greed—and so I unlatched another suitcase to see if there was even more.

Unfortunately, the next suitcase I opened had something less valauble and more volatile. Strapped inside was a mess of wires and small canisters, all of which were hooked up to a digital interface that said "Ready to arm." The greed in my heart was very suddenly replaced with unbridled terror. I jumped back and slammed into the dashboard, scrambling out of the car and throwing the door closed. My breath fell out heavy from my lips, my heart panicked and racing.

Just to ensure that I didn't accidentally activate it, I cracked the door open a smidge and peered inside. Thankfully, the interface on the explosive hadn't changed, so I let out a very thankful sigh of relief.
