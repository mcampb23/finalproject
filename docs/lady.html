The Carwash Caper

I had to find that lady.

I didn't want to get myself involved in any kind of press coverage or legal drama that would ultimately result from my call, so I decided to take matters into my own hands to resolve it quietly.

I very seldom went up to the main part of the building. Dressed as I was in a jumpsuit, I would stand out like a sore thumb up in the lobby, but that wasn't something I gave much headspace. I took the utility elevator up to the main floor and wandered into the lobby, gawking at the beautiful scenery and decorations littering the entire chamber. Even though it was past midnight, there was still plenty of activity going on—there was some kind of high-profile wedding happening that night—which afforded me a good number of awkward stares.

I didn't want to mingle in with the crowd too much, but a lady dressed the way she was wouldn't have been that hard to spot. However, another part of me really didn't want to get in the way of everything going on. Whoever was working the front desk that night would have seen everything, right?
