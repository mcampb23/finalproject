The Carwash Caper

I didn't put up with it.

I had only worked at this carwash for a few months, but working the night shift taught me how to deal with her type when they showed up.

"Look," I began. "If you can't pay upfront, then I can't do anything for you. Simple as that."
"Then how about I go ahead and do it myself if it's going to be that much of a problem?" she responded.
"I thought you had somewhere to be?" I retorted.
"And I would have been up there by now if you just let me go. There's nothing wrong with giving people a little leeway sometimes, little girl."
"And there's nothing wrong with following the rules. So what's it gonna be?"
"If that's how it's going to be, how about I just leave my car here? Trust me, I'll be back quicker than you think. No need to wash it... but if you want to, I won't stop you."

I couldn't help but sigh at how stubborn she was. Even my own mother wasn't this pushy. We clearly weren't getting anywhere...
